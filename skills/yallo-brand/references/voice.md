# YALLO Voice, Tone & Copy Guidelines

## Brand Character

YALLO sounds like a **chief architect presenting at a whiteboard**: calm, confident, clear, and very specific.
Not a recruiter. Not a consultant. An architect who also happens to find the right people.

---

## Tone principles

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Specific and direct | Vague or generic |
| Confident without arrogance | Boastful or sales-y |
| Technical but human | Jargon-heavy |
| Sentence case | ALL CAPS headlines |
| Short sentences (max 2 lines per heading) | Run-on copy |
| Numbers and proof | Buzzwords without substance |
| "We deliver" | "We leverage synergies" |

## Audience — who we write for

**Primary:** CIO · CTO · IT Director · Programme Director · VP of Technology
**Secondary:** HR Director · Procurement · PMO Lead

**What they care about:**
- Programme delivery speed
- Risk management
- Quality of technical talent
- Not wasting time on irrelevant CVs
- UAE / GCC talent availability
- Compliance and contractual clarity

**What they don't want to hear:**
- "We have thousands of candidates in our database"
- "Best-in-class solutions"
- "Holistic approach to recruitment"
- Any exclamation marks

---

## Headline formulas that work for YALLO

1. **Stat + context:** "72 hours. Not 2 weeks."
2. **Problem + solution:** "Your programme launch is at risk. We fix that."
3. **Proof statement:** "1,000+ engineers placed. Zero compromises."
4. **Question that stings:** "Still waiting for the right SAP consultant?"
5. **Contrast:** "Generic CVs. Or architect-vetted profiles. Your choice."

---

## StoryBrand structure (for landing pages / decks)

1. **Character (hero):** CIO / Programme Director with a technology delivery gap
2. **Problem:**
   - External: Can't find specialist IT talent fast enough
   - Internal: Frustration — wrong CVs, wasted time, delayed programmes
   - Philosophical: Enterprise programmes deserve precision, not guesswork
3. **Guide:** YALLO — 20+ years of experience, architect-led, not just a recruiter
4. **Plan:** Brief us → Architect review → 2 vetted profiles within 72 hours → Place
5. **Call to action:** "Brief us today" / "Get profiles in 72 hours"
6. **Success:** Programme on track. Right talent. On time.
7. **Failure (stakes):** 6–12 months delivery delay. Programme risk. Budget overrun.

## Presentation copy rules

- Headlines: Plus Jakarta Sans ExtraBold 800, sentence case
- Max 6 words in a slide headline
- Body: max 3 bullet points per content slide
- Proof stats always gold
- Never use "we" more than once per slide
- Footer every slide: YALLO · yallo.co

---

## Email copy rules

- Subject lines: under 50 characters
- No "I hope this email finds you well"
- Open with a specific observation or stat
- One ask per email
- Sign off: "Best, [Name]" — never "Kind regards" in client-facing
- Always include yallo.co
